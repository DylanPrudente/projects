Change Blindness Explanation

Explanation:

Change blindness is a cognitive phenomenon where individuals fail to notice substantial changes in a visual scene, particularly when the change coincides with a visual disruption (like a blank screen or flicker). This highlights the limits of visual attention and our brainâ€™s reliance on high-level expectations rather than detailed, moment-by-moment visual tracking.

In this task, the blank "mask" or buffer between image transitions disrupts the visual continuity, making it harder for the brain to detect differences. As the buffer duration decreases, the illusion is weakened, allowing the observer to more easily identify the change. This demonstrates how attention is necessary for conscious perception of change and how our experience of a stable visual world is, in part, a constructed illusion.
