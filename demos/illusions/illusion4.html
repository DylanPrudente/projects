Kanizsa Triangle

The Kanizsa Triangle is a classic example of illusory contours, where our brain fills in missing edges to perceive a shape that doesn’t physically exist. Even though there are only three “Pac-Man” shapes and three angles, most people clearly see a bright white triangle floating above them. This illusion teaches us that the brain prefers simple, complete forms and engages in gestalt processing—constructing whole objects from partial information. It demonstrates how we are wired to organize ambiguous visual data into meaningful patterns.
