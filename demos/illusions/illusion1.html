Müller-Lyer's Illusion

The Müller-Lyer illusion demonstrates how context affects perceived length. Two lines of equal length appear drastically different when arrow-like fins are placed at their ends, pointing inward or outward. This illusion highlights the role of perspective cues in spatial judgment. Our brains interpret the “inward” fins as representing the inside corner of a room (receding away), while the “outward” fins resemble an outside corner (jutting out). This visual trick reveals how top-down processing—our brain’s interpretation based on prior knowledge—can override actual physical measurements.
