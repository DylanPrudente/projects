Rotating Snakes Illusion

The Rotating Snakes illusion creates the perception of motion in static images, especially when we shift our gaze or blink. This illusion reveals how microsaccades—tiny, involuntary eye movements—interact with patterned stimuli to create motion. The brain attempts to resolve conflicting visual signals by interpreting them as movement. It teaches us about motion detection mechanisms and how easily the brain can be tricked into perceiving movement even without actual displacement.
